About Team Hopper

Grace Hopper (1906-1992) was a pioneer of computer programming and was the first to devise the theory of machine-independent programming languages.

While she was working on a Mark II Computer at Harvard University in 1947, her associates discovered a moth that was stuck in a relay and impending the operation of the computer. Upon extraction, the insect was affixed to a log sheet for that day with the notation, "First actual case of a bug being found".

The case is held as a historical instance of 'debugging' a computer and Hopper is credited with popularising the term in computing.

As we've done a lot of debugging during this project, we thought it was an apt name for our team.
